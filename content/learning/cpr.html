Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR) Instruction

I am an American Heart Association (AHA) certified CPR instructor and I teach CPR for professionals (BLS) and lay responders (Heartstart).

I also offer mass-classes for free, for example for schools. Please contact me if you would like more info.

Basic Life Support (BLS)

Basic life support, or BLS, is intended for professionals who work in the healthcare field and need to be trained to respond in an emergency.

Heartstart

Heartstart CPR is intended for anyone who wants to be able to respond in an emergency. It is great when combined with first aid training.

Basic Life Support (BLS)

Basic life support teaches the skills to keep someone alive after their heart has stopped. It differs from heartstart CPR in that it also teaches airway management skills.

This course is intended for anyone with a higher chance of encountering a patient whose heart has stopped for medical reasons, such as life guards, emergency medical technicians, and non-medical staff at medical facilities. It is also necessary, along with Advanced Cardiac Life Support (ACLS) for medical providers, such as nurses, paramedics, physician assistants, and physicians.

Heartstart CPR

Heartstart CPR teaches you how to do CPR on any adult or child of any age. All people should know CPR. It can save the people you love and give you the power to help strangers in need.

I teach this course at a discounted rate to encourage more people to be trained. I also am willing to teach it for free in some situations, although I cannot provide a certification if it is taught for free.

This class is 100% in-person. If done for a certification, some portions are taught via a pre-recorded video from the AHA. If no certification is needed, the course is taught entirely by me and at a discounted rate (the cost of the certification from the AHA is $20).
