Learn Wilderness Medicine!

Going camping? Climbing? Skiing? All of the above at the same time? Make sure you are ready for emergencies in sitations where emergency medical help can be a very long way away!

I have been teaching wilderness medicine since 2014. I have taught many wilderness first aid weekends and helped run a medical-school quarter-long course on wilderness medicine. I have planned and executed trips where I have put 80 people into the wilderness and I have provided emergency medical care in the wilderness myself.

My courses are intended to be customized to your needs. If you aren't going diving, there is no need to learn dive medicine, if you aren't going to climb massive mountains, there is no need to learn the management of altitude sickness in detail. Or maybe you are a doctor or other healthcare provider and want to learn how to apply your skills in the wilderness in more detail in which case we can cover everything!

You can learn wilderness medicine with your friends and family in an environment tailored to your learning objectives, so sign up below!

And yes, I can teach you how to rescue a cold dog from the snow {{}}.

Core Wilderness Medicine Topics To Mix-n-Match

Altitude

How does altitude affect the human body? Which symptoms should you worry about and which don't really matter? How can you avoid getting sick when climbing a mountain to maximize fun?

{{}}

Water

Going rafting? Backingpacking somewhere with river crossings? Just scared of water? Learn how to deal with water emergencies, drowning, trauma, and hypothermia to increase your confidence.

{{}}

Fractures

Go beyond first aid and learn how to split sprains and fractures and evacuate someone even though they have a broken bone, and how to provide excellent care with minimal tools.

{{}}

Cold

How should you treat frostbite? What about hypothermia? How do you even know if someone has hypothermia? Learn how to be prepared on your winter adventures.

{{}}

Heat

Learn how to handle the heat. Should you drink water all day? What about electrolytes? What is the fastest way to cool someone down? When should you do it?

{{}}

Evacuation

When should you evacuate? When should you call for someone to come to you? Practice staging an evacuation using just the things you have with you.

{{}}

CPR

Need to learn CPR too? Want to know when CPR is valuable in the wilderness and when it isn't? Bundle a CPR class, with or without a certification, with your wilderness training!

{{}}

Wounds

Want to know how to clean and dress wounds? Stop severe bleeding? Gain practical experience with a variety of wound care techniques so that you are ready for small and large injuries alike!

{{}}

Planning

Learn how to safely plan your trip. How do you get good maps on your phone? Should you have print maps? How do you use them? What should you pack?

{{}}

Frequently Asked Questions

How many people per class?

The minimum per group is 3, max is 10–20, depending on complexity

Can I sign up alone?

I do hold group classes a few times a year depending on interest. If you would like to be added to the wait list please let me know.

If you want to do a one or two person class, that is ok, but you will need to pay for the smallest available class below.

How are the classes taught?

I have an online portal that I use to assign lectures and other content prior to the day (unless you don't want that), and then in-person classes are somewhere in the wilderness—generally one of the bigger parks in the bay area, but other locations are possible.

Do I need to bring anything?

Yes. You will need to bring your backpacking supplies to practice with (I provide what you don't have) plus food and water for one or two days outside. I'll give you more information after you sign up.

How long are the courses?

They range from 6 hours to a full weekend see below.
