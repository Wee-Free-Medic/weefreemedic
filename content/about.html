This project was born of my twin interests in teaching and medical care for the underserved.

I have long been interested in being part of the solution to the broken US medical system. The company-provided insurance model falls apart completely when people's lives become sufficiently broken that they can no longer work—generally this is also when they stop purchasing insurance, which is so expensive. This is exactly the time when people need the most help.

I also enjoy teaching emergency medical skills to everyone who needs them—which is almost everyone.

I created Wee Free Medic (named for the {{}} of {{}}, a group of independent and unusual individuals who decided to try and make their town a better place) to allow me to provide medical care to those who need it, accept donations, obtain naloxone from the state, and teach the skills I love to teach.

This project will evolve as I do. When I become a licensed physician, I will expand the range of care I can provide.

Medical Care

Medical care should be easy to obtain. I provide multiple services aimed at making it that way.

Street medicine is the core of my mission and my primary volunteer activity. My other work here largely supports that. I also have extensive experience providing medical care in event contexts.

  • Free medical care if you are uninsured or homeless
  • Comprehensive event medical support, in any environment
  • Discounts available if your event has a mission

Teaching

Trained and experienced medical skills instructor. Learn the skills you need.

I have many years of experience teaching CPR, first aid, and wilderness medicine. I specialize in creating customized classes for you and your group to learn the skills to keep yourselves safe.

  • Deep understanding of CPR and cardiovascular anatomy
  • Experience providing medical care in the wilderness
  • Discounts available if needed, contact me to explain your need

Mike Dacre

I have been a wilderness medicine certified provider since 2012 and an emergency medical technician (EMT) since 2014. Since that time I have worked throughout the bay area and as a wilderness-EMT with the {{}}. I am now a {{}} graduating in 2023. I am planning on going into Family Medicine when I graduate.

I have been teaching wilderness medicine since 2014 and CPR as an AHA-certified instructor since 2018. Teaching is a passion of mine and I love to do it. My classes are dynamic and energetic and I pride myself on matching the level of the content to the level of the learner.

I started practicing street medicine independently in 2022 because I felt my medical skill had reached the level necessary for this work and because the pandemic has ballooned the number of unhoused people. Furthermore, I am very aware of the devastating effects of super-strong fentanyl and tainted p2p methamphatamine in our community, particularly affecting those most abandoned by society.
